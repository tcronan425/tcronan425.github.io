Polar Bears

Where polar bears are found

Polar bears are commonly found in the artic region. They commonly live on sea ice and are most abundant in Northern canada. Polar bears arent found in antartica and their habitat greatly relys on the sea ice for them to hunt seals on.

Characteristics

Some physical characteristics of a polor bear are their fur. Polar bears have white fur which serves as camouflage in their arctic habitat. They also have black skin which helps them absorb the sun and keeps them warm. Polar bears are also very large they can be up to 10 feet long and weight up to 1000 pounds.

Adaptations

Some adaptations of polar bears are their webbed paws. Polar bears have webbed paws which make them pretty good swimmers. This helsp them travel to different pieces of ice and hunt animals such as seals. They also have a thick layer of fat which is used to keep them warm in the harsh artic climate. Polar bears also have a small ears adn tail. This adaptation is used to keep them warm as smaller ears and tails help minimize heat loss helping keep them warm.
